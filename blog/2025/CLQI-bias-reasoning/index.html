This is just a quick post, but the reason why CLQI was producing biased results was because I was drawing random values from $u \sim \text{Uniform}(0,0.99)$ and not $u \sim \text{Uniform}(0,1)$. The reason I did this was because I was doing interpolation assuming that data cannot be shared across studies. This tiny error has been causing me a massive headache, and I’m finally glad I recognized and fixed this mistake. Now I can actually go through the simulations for this method and testing its real performance :)
